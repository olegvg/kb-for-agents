Just finished reading "Cognitive Architectures for Language Agents" (https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.02427).

Several ideas here transfer directly to agent pipeline design, even though that wasn't the paper's framing. The evaluation methodology is worth borrowing, separate from the results. I disagreed with one assumption in the setup, but the conclusions are robust enough to survive it. Applying this to context-window management could cut token usage by a meaningful fraction. The technique maps cleanly onto the notes-corpus ingestion pipeline I'm designing.
