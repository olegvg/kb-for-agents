Just finished reading "Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior" (https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03442).

Several ideas here transfer directly to agent pipeline design, even though that wasn't the paper's framing. I disagreed with one assumption in the setup, but the conclusions are robust enough to survive it. Applying this to context-window management could cut token usage by a meaningful fraction. I'll try the main heuristic on a real codebase next week to see if it generalises. The approach suggests a way to audit legacy code systematically rather than heuristically.
