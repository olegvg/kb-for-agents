Just finished reading "Voyager: An Open-Ended Embodied Agent with Large Language Models" (https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.16291).

The evaluation methodology is worth borrowing, separate from the results. This reframed how I think about the knowledge freshness problem in retrieval systems. I had heard the headline finding cited dozens of times, but reading the actual method section changed my interpretation. The pattern shows up in how RAG-vs-compiled-knowledge tradeoffs should actually be evaluated. The approach suggests a way to audit legacy code systematically rather than heuristically.
