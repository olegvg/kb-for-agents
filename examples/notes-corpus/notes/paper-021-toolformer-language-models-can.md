Just finished reading "Toolformer: Language Models Can Teach Themselves to Use Tools" (https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.04761).

I disagreed with one assumption in the setup, but the conclusions are robust enough to survive it. The core claim held up better than I expected when I applied it to my own setup. Applying this to context-window management could cut token usage by a meaningful fraction. The approach suggests a way to audit legacy code systematically rather than heuristically. I'll try the main heuristic on a real codebase next week to see if it generalises.
