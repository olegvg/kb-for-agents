Just finished reading "HyDE: Hypothetical Document Embeddings for Zero-Shot Dense Retrieval" (https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10496).

The ablation tables are where the real insight lives — the abstract undersells the nuance. The failure cases section was more useful to me than the success cases. The evaluation methodology is worth borrowing, separate from the results. The approach suggests a way to audit legacy code systematically rather than heuristically.
