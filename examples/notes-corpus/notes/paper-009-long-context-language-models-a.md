Just finished reading "Long-Context Language Models and Context Window Management (synthetic)" (https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.00001).

The evaluation methodology is worth borrowing, separate from the results. This reframed how I think about the knowledge freshness problem in retrieval systems. I had heard the headline finding cited dozens of times, but reading the actual method section changed my interpretation. The technique maps cleanly onto the notes-corpus ingestion pipeline I'm designing. I can see a direct application to how I structure context for long agent sessions.
