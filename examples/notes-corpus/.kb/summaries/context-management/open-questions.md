# Open Questions on Context and Knowledge Management

The idea notes in this corpus record unresolved hypotheses — things noticed but not yet tested. They are lighter-weight than tool notes (no "what worked / what didn't" structure) and are meant to age on the shelf until a project makes them actionable.

## Compiled knowledge staleness

`notes/idea-000-i-keep-wondering-whether-compi.md` and its duplicate `notes/idea-014-i-keep-wondering-whether-compi.md` ask: does compiled knowledge degrade slower than retrieval indexes? The intuition is that a well-written summary remains useful longer than an embedding index over the same source, because the summary distills intent rather than surface tokens. Not tested.

## Fresh vs stale dimension

`notes/idea-001-what-if-the-fresh-vs-stale-d.md` / `notes/idea-015-what-if-the-fresh-vs-stale-d.md` propose that the freshness axis should be treated as a first-class dimension in knowledge architecture — not a footnote about cache invalidation. The hypothesis: if knowledge is tagged with a freshness score, agents can prefer stale-but-reliable over fresh-but-noisy when the task calls for it.

## Confidence scoring

`notes/idea-006-what-if-every-note-had-a-confi.md` / `notes/idea-020-what-if-every-note-had-a-confi.md` ask whether attaching confidence scores to notes (updated over time as claims are confirmed or refuted) would make the corpus self-curating. Related to the agent-uncertainty idea in `notes/idea-013-what-if-the-agent-s-own-uncert.md`.

## Context-window limits as a design constraint

`notes/idea-011-there-s-an-open-question-about.md` flags an unresolved question about whether context-window limits are a temporary engineering constraint (to be solved by larger windows) or a permanent architectural signal (forcing good knowledge design regardless of window size). The thesis notes in this domain generally treat them as the latter.
